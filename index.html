Ground motion model evaluation

We are doing some exciting things with visualizing and evaluating the performance of ground motion prediction equations (GMPEs), including tracking model performance over time and visualizing model errors spatially. Click above to test out our application of interactive visualizations of GMPE residuals and scores, for 14 GMPEs for Japan. Some preliminary results for this evaluation experiment are presented here.

Intensity prediction equation evaluation

Macroseismic intensity is a measure often used to quantify site-specific damage of earthquakes. Click above for an interactive application that visualizes residuals and other evaluation scores for ten intensity prediction equations (IPEs) for Italy. The IPEs are tested against several observation databases, including USGS Did You Feel It (DYFI) questionnaires from the Italian region. We are also evaluating a global IPE using global DYFI data.

Completeness

Catalog completeness is an important component of estimating parameters of earthquake and ground motion models. However, most estimation methods rely on the Gutenberg-Richter distribution. Click above to learn about our Probabilistic Magnitude of Completeness method - a distribution-free estimation method - and construct completeness maps. More details and results can be found here.

Maximum magnitude testing

The maximum magnitude (Mmax) is a crucial parameter for seismic hazard assessment, but its accuracy remains untestable. Click above to try out our application for estimating the number of earthquakes and number of years required in order to test with high power a hypothesized value of Mmax. Details and results are presented here.

Earthquake rate forecast evaluation

Before we can calculate seismic hazard, a well-tested forecast for earthquake rates is necessary. We use a variety of statistical methods, broadly grouped into 2 types, to find the best forecast in evaluation experiments around the globe. Click here for a slideshow that shows our techniques in action.

An operational testing center

The testing center is designed for open, reproducible and prospective testing and evaluation of intensity and ground motion models. Ground motion models and data are collected and stored, a suite of scores and evaluations are run on ground motion models, and all results are saved - all without human interference. For more details, see here and here.
